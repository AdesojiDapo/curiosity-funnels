**Screenshot brief:**
1. Open ChatGPT. Ask: *"Tell me about [pick any small UK company you know, ideally not a customer]. Founders, what they do, their pricing if known."*

3. Circle (in red) one factual error or one "I don't have information about..." line.
4. Blur the company name with a black bar so it's anonymous.

The screenshot is what drives the click. Without it the post is a tweet, with it it's a story.

## B. LinkedIn DMs (template + 4 trigger variants)

You won't write 20 unique DMs. You'll fill one template 20 times. The personalisation is one line at the top that proves you actually looked at them.

### Core template

```
Hi [first name].

[ONE PERSONALISATION LINE, see below.]

Have you ever actually asked ChatGPT what it says about [Company]? Most owners haven't, and the answers are weirder than you'd expect. Built a 90-second thing that shows you across four AI tools, £99, report in 48 hours:

https://funnels.curiositybyade.com/f/ai-visibility-audit

No reply needed unless you want to talk.

Adesoji
```

### Personalisation line, four triggers

Pick the one that fits what their profile shows.

**Trigger A — they posted about AI in the last 30 days**
> Saw your post on [topic, in 3-5 words] last week, properly agreed with the [bit about X].

**Trigger B — recent rebrand, redesign, or launch**
> Noticed the new [site / logo / product] is live. Looks good.

**Trigger C — recent funding, milestone, or hire**
> Saw the [Series A / new CMO / 5-year anniversary] news, congrats.

**Trigger D — no signal, you just want to reach them**
> Followed for a while, [specific thing you respect about how they run [Company]].

If you can't write Trigger D honestly, skip the person. Cold DMs that open with fake admiration get ignored.

### Personalisation rules

- First name only, never "Hi there".
- Their company name in the second paragraph, exactly as they spell it.
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am or 2pm-4pm UK time. Skip Monday morning and Friday afternoon.
- No follow-up if they don't reply. The link does the follow-up. Chasing kills conversion.
- If they reply with a question, answer the question, then drop the link again at the end of your answer. Don't push for a call.

### How to find 20 in 30 minutes

LinkedIn search filters:
- **Location:** United Kingdom
- **Industry:** pick one (Financial Services, Marketing & Advertising, Management Consulting, Legal Services)
- **Current Company Size:** 1-10 or 11-50
- **Title contains:** Founder, Owner, Managing Director, CEO

Scroll. Pick 20 where you can write Trigger A/B/C/D honestly. Save each to a Sales Navigator list, or just a Notion table with columns: name, company, trigger, sent? Y/N, replied? Y/N.

```

https://funnels.curiositybyade.com/f/ai-visibility-audit
```

That's the bar. Don't send a DM unless you can write a line that specific.

### Don'ts

- Don't say "quick question". They know it isn't quick.
- Don't paste a brochure.
- Don't ask for a call. The funnel does the qualifying.

### Cold email rules

- One email per prospect per week. Maximum two follow-ups, then move on.
- Send 5-15 per day, not 50. Above 15 you trip spam filters and burn the curiositybyade.com domain reputation.
- Each email should mention something specific to them in the first or last sentence. If you can't, send the LinkedIn DM instead.
- Track sends in a spreadsheet: sent date, opened (free tracker like Mailtrack), replied, paid.

### Subject lines, ranked

By likely open rate:
1. *"ChatGPT got [Company] wrong"* — direct, specific, curiosity
2. *"A competitor of yours is being recommended by ChatGPT"* — fear
3. *"A second pair of eyes on [yourcompany.com]"* — soft, helpful
4. *"Building [Company]'s next website"* — only works for warm prospects
5. *"Buyers ask ChatGPT before they ask you"* — slightly generic, save for fallback

Rotate. Whichever drives the most replies, double down for week two.

## D. Free communities to post in (5 places)

Read the rules in each. Self-promotion is fine in some, banned in others. Match the post to the rules.

1. **Indie Hackers (https://indiehackers.com/)**
   - Post in "Growth" or "Marketing". Frame as a learning-in-public post, not a sales pitch.
   - "Built a £99 AI visibility audit. Here's what 10 customers taught me about how ChatGPT describes small businesses."
2. **r/smallbusiness (UK and US)**
   - Strict no-promo rules. Post only when answering a question where the audit is the genuine answer. Don't lead with the link, lead with the help.
3. **r/digitalmarketing**
   - Looser rules. Frame as a case study. Share one real (anonymised) audit finding, then the link.
4. **UK-specific Slack groups**
   - "Founders Network UK", "Tech London", "Bristol Tech". Search "UK founders Slack". Most have a #self-promo channel.
5. **LinkedIn comments under big posts**
   - Find posts about AI from people with 50k+ followers, in your target industries. Leave a thoughtful comment that mentions the audit naturally, with the link. Reach often beats the original post's reach for the commenter.

## G. Don'ts

- Don't write "I built an AI tool that..." in any post. Boring. Lead with the result.
- Don't post about the funnel itself. Nobody cares. Post about the buyer's problem.
- Don't run paid ads until at least 10 organic leads convert. You need to know the funnel works first.
- Don't change the funnel mid-week. Let it run. Judge it at the end of week one.

## H. Week-one success bar

By Sunday next week:
- 200+ funnel impressions (page loads)
- 50+ funnel starts (cover CTA click)
- 10+ completed leads (email submitted)
- 1+ paid customer

If we hit those numbers, the funnel and channels work. We scale.
If we hit impressions and starts but not leads, the funnel is wrong. We rewrite questions.
If we don't hit impressions, distribution is wrong. We test different posts.
